# Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers Index (Through Augustine)

## Purpose

The **Nicene and Post-Nicene era** (325-~500 AD) represents the "golden age" of patristic theology—the great councils, precise doctrinal formulations, and towering figures like Athanasius, the Cappadocians, Jerome, Ambrose, and Augustine. This index focuses through Augustine (d. 430), honoring the project's scope while noting key contemporaries.

---

## Historical Context

### Major Councils

| Council | Date | Key Issue | Definition |
|---------|------|-----------|------------|
| **Nicaea I** | 325 | Arianism | Christ *homoousios* (same substance) with Father |
| **Constantinople I** | 381 | Arianism/Pneumatomachi | Nicene Creed finalized; Spirit's divinity |
| **Ephesus** | 431 | Nestorianism | *Theotokos* affirmed |
| **Chalcedon** | 451 | Eutychianism | Two natures, one Person in Christ |

### Major Heresies Addressed

| Heresy | Error | Orthodox Response |
|--------|-------|-------------------|
| **Arianism** | Christ is created | Nicaea: *homoousios* |
| **Pneumatomachians** | Spirit is created | Constantinople: Spirit worshipped |
| **Apollinarianism** | Christ has no human mind | Christ fully human |
| **Nestorianism** | Two persons in Christ | One Person, *Theotokos* |
| **Pelagianism** | Grace unnecessary | Augustine: grace essential |
| **Donatism** | Sacraments depend on minister | Sacraments valid objectively |

## Athanasius of Alexandria (~296-373)

### Basic Information

**Life**: Deacon at Nicaea (325); Bishop of Alexandria (328); exiled 5 times for defending Nicaea; "Athanasius contra mundum"  
**Significance**: Champion of Nicene orthodoxy; greatest anti-Arian Father

### Major Works

| Work | Type | Content |
|------|------|---------|
| **On the Incarnation** (*De Incarnatione*) | Apologetic/Dogmatic | Why God became man |
| **Orations Against the Arians** (3-4) | Polemical | Detailed anti-Arian arguments |
| **Life of Antony** | Hagiography | Father of monks; desert spirituality |
| **Festal Letters** | Pastoral | Annual Easter letters (Letter 39 on canon) |
| **Letters to Serapion** | Dogmatic | Holy Spirit's divinity |

#### On the Incarnation (~318 AD)

**Purpose**: Explain why Logos became flesh  
**Audience**: Convert Macarius

**Structure**:
1. Creation and Fall (1-10)
2. The Divine Dilemma (11-16)
3. The Incarnation's purpose (17-32)
4. Resurrection proofs (33-40)
5. Against Jews and Greeks (41-57)

**Key Passages**:

**Section 54** (Foundational for theosis):
> "He became man that we might become god (*theopoiēthōmen*); and he manifested himself through a body that we might receive an idea of the invisible Father; and he endured the insults of men that we might inherit immortality."

**Section 8-9** (Divine dilemma):
God faced a dilemma: let humanity perish (inconsistent with goodness) or ignore sin (inconsistent with truth). The Incarnation solves both.

**Section 20** (Why death by cross):
> "He surrendered his body to death in place of all, and offered it to the Father."

#### Orations Against the Arians

Detailed refutation of Arian arguments. Key themes:

- Son is eternally begotten, not created
- "There was not when he was not" is false
- Scripture texts apparently supporting Arianism explained

**Oration 1.9** (Eternal generation):
> "The Son is other than things that came to be; he alone is proper to the Father's substance."

#### Life of Antony (~357 AD)

**Significance**: Foundational monastic text; influenced Augustine's conversion

Antony (d. 356) as model of ascetic life, spiritual combat, and contemplation.

### Letters to Serapion

Defends divinity of Holy Spirit against "Tropici" (Spirit-deniers).

### Festal Letter 39 (367 AD)

**Significance**: First listing of 27 NT books as canonical—standard list used today.

### Where to Find Full Text

- **New Advent**: newadvent.org/fathers (search "Athanasius")
- **CCEL**: ccel.org/ccel/schaff/npnf204 (NPNF2 Volume 4)
- **Early Church Texts**: earlychurchtexts.com/main/athanasius/

## The Cappadocian Fathers

Three friends and relatives from Cappadocia who completed Trinitarian theology.

### Basil the Great (~330-379)

**Life**: Bishop of Caesarea; monastic organizer; liturgical contributor  
**Role**: Practical leader; monastic rule; liturgy

#### Major Works

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| **On the Holy Spirit** | Spirit's divinity and worship |
| **Against Eunomius** | Refuting extreme Arianism |
| **Hexaemeron** (6 Days) | Homilies on creation |
| **Monastic Rules** | Long Rules, Short Rules |
| **Letters** | 368 surviving; theology and pastoral |
| **Liturgy of St. Basil** | Eucharistic liturgy (still used) |

**On the Holy Spirit** (Key for Pneumatology):
> "We worship the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit, confessing each Person to be distinct, yet not dividing the Godhead."

**Hexaemeron** (On creation):
Literal and spiritual exposition of Genesis 1; influential for natural theology.

### Gregory of Nazianzus (~329-390)

**Life**: Reluctant bishop; briefly Patriarch of Constantinople (381); "The Theologian"  
**Role**: Greatest orator; Trinitarian precision

#### Major Works

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Five Theological Orations** | Orations 27-31; definitive Trinitarian theology |
| **Other Orations** (45 total) | Festal, funeral, occasional |
| **Letters** | 249 surviving |
| **Poems** | Extensive religious poetry |

**Theological Orations** (380 AD):

**Oration 27**: Proper approach to theology (humility)  
**Oration 28**: Knowledge of God; what we can know  
**Oration 29**: The Son; against Eunomians  
**Oration 30**: More on the Son; biblical texts  
**Oration 31**: The Holy Spirit

**Oration 29.2** (On the Son's begetting):
> "The Father is unbegotten, the Son is begotten, the Holy Spirit proceeds."

**Oration 31.9** (Spirit):
> "What then? Is the Spirit God? Most certainly. Well then, is he consubstantial? Yes, if he is God."

### Gregory of Nyssa (~335-395)

**Life**: Bishop of Nyssa; younger brother of Basil; mystic theologian  
**Role**: Speculative theologian; spiritual writer

#### Major Works

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Against Eunomius** (12 books) | Anti-Arian theology |
| **Great Catechism** | Doctrinal instruction |
| **Life of Moses** | Spiritual exegesis; mystical theology |
| **On the Soul and Resurrection** | Dialogue with sister Macrina |
| **On Virginity** | Spiritual life |
| **Homilies on the Song of Songs** | Mystical interpretation |
| **Life of Macrina** | Biography of his saintly sister |

**Life of Moses** (Foundational for mystical theology):
Moses' journey as allegory of soul's ascent to God. The "divine darkness"—knowing God by unknowing.

**On the Soul and Resurrection**:
Dialogue with dying sister Macrina on immortality, resurrection, restoration.

**Great Catechism** (Important for catechetics):
Systematic summary of Christian faith for instructing converts.

### Cappadocian Contributions

| Doctrine | Contribution |
|----------|--------------|
| **Trinity** | Distinguished *ousia* (essence) from *hypostasis* (person) |
| **Pneumatology** | Spirit's full divinity |
| **Christology** | Full humanity; against Apollinaris |
| **Mystical theology** | Especially Gregory of Nyssa |
| **Monasticism** | Basil's rules |

### Where to Find Full Text

- **New Advent**: newadvent.org/fathers (search each name)
- **CCEL**: NPNF2 Volume 5 (Gregory of Nyssa); Volume 7 (Basil, Gregory Naz.)

## Ephrem the Syrian (~306-373)

### Basic Information

**Life**: Deacon at Nisibis, then Edessa; Syriac tradition  
**Significance**: Greatest Syriac poet; "Harp of the Spirit"; Marian hymns

### Major Works

| Work | Type |
|------|------|
| **Hymns on Paradise** | Theological poetry |
| **Hymns on the Nativity** | Christmas cycle |
| **Hymns on Faith** | Doctrinal |
| **Hymns Against Heresies** | Anti-Arian, anti-Gnostic |
| **Commentaries** | Genesis, Exodus, Diatessaron |
| **Nisibene Hymns** | Historical/occasional |

### Marian Content

Ephrem's hymns are rich in Marian imagery:

**Hymn on the Nativity 11**:
> "Mary became a heaven for us, because she bore the divinity... The Lord entered her and became a servant."

**On Paradise, Hymn 6**:
Mary and Eve typology developed poetically.

### Where to Find Full Text

- **CCEL**: ccel.org (Nicene and Post-Nicene Fathers, Series II)
- Academic: Sebastian Brock translations

## Jerome (~347-420)

### Basic Information

**Life**: Born in Stridon; educated in Rome; desert ascetic; Bethlehem monastery  
**Significance**: Greatest biblical scholar; Vulgate translator; combative personality

### Major Works

| Work | Type | Content |
|------|------|---------|
| **Vulgate** | Translation | Latin Bible from Hebrew/Greek |
| **Commentaries** | Exegetical | Most of OT and NT |
| **De Viris Illustribus** | Historical | Early Christian writers |
| **Against Helvidius** | Polemical | Mary's perpetual virginity |
| **Against Jovinian** | Polemical | Virginity and marriage |
| **Letters** | Correspondence | 154 surviving |

#### The Vulgate

**Significance**: Standard Latin Bible for over 1000 years

**Method**: Translated OT from Hebrew (unprecedented); revised NT from Greek.

#### Against Helvidius (~383 AD)

**Defense of Mary's perpetual virginity**

Key arguments:
- "Brothers of Jesus" are cousins (Aramaic usage)
- Joseph was guardian, not husband in full sense
- "Until" (Matt 1:25) doesn't imply "after"
- Mary's question to Gabriel (Luke 1:34) implies vow

> "You say that Mary did not remain a virgin. I claim more: that Joseph himself was a virgin through Mary."

#### Against Jovinian (~393 AD)

Defends superiority of virginity (while affirming marriage's goodness); extensive Marian content.

#### Commentaries

Jerome wrote on most biblical books. Known for:
- Hebrew scholarship
- Historical-grammatical sense alongside spiritual
- Occasional allegory

### Where to Find Full Text

- **New Advent**: newadvent.org/fathers (search "Jerome")
- **CCEL**: NPNF2 Volume 6
- **Tertullian Project**: tertullian.org/fathers (good Jerome collection)

## Ambrose of Milan (~340-397)

### Basic Information

**Life**: Governor who became Bishop of Milan (374); baptized Augustine (387)  
**Significance**: Western patriarch; influenced Augustine; hymnodist; defender of Church independence

### Major Works

| Work | Type | Content |
|------|------|---------|
| **De Officiis** | Ethics | Christian duties (modeled on Cicero) |
| **De Sacramentis** | Catechetical | Baptism, Eucharist explanations |
| **De Mysteriis** | Catechetical | Similar to above |
| **De Fide** | Dogmatic | Against Arians |
| **De Spiritu Sancto** | Dogmatic | Holy Spirit's divinity |
| **De Virginibus** | Ascetical | On virgins (to his sister) |
| **Hexaemeron** | Exegetical | On creation (after Basil) |
| **Hymns** | Liturgical | Foundational Latin hymnody |

#### De Sacramentis & De Mysteriis

**Key for sacramental theology**:

**De Sacramentis IV.14** (Eucharistic change):
> "Before the words of Christ the cup is full of wine and water; when the words of Christ have been added, then it is made the blood of Christ."

**De Mysteriis 54** (Real presence):
> "Perhaps you say: 'I see something else; how do you tell me that I receive the Body of Christ?' ... The word of Christ... can change things created into what they were not."

#### Against Arianism

**De Fide** (5 books): Systematic anti-Arian theology written for Emperor Gratian.

### Where to Find Full Text

- **New Advent**: newadvent.org/fathers (search "Ambrose")
- **CCEL**: NPNF2 Volume 10

## Augustine of Hippo (354-430)

### Basic Information

**Life**: Born in Thagaste (N. Africa); Manichee, then Neo-Platonist; converted 386 (Milan); baptized 387; Bishop of Hippo 396-430  
**Significance**: Most influential Western Father; shaped medieval and Reformation theology

### Major Works

#### Autobiographical

| Work | Date | Content |
|------|------|---------|
| **Confessions** | ~397-400 | Autobiography and praise; books 1-9 narrative, 10-13 philosophical |

**Confessions** key passages:

**I.1.1**:
> "You have made us for yourself, O Lord, and our hearts are restless until they rest in you."

**VIII.12.29** (Conversion):
> "Take up and read, take up and read... I snatched it up, opened it, and in silence read the passage on which my eyes first fell: 'Not in riots and drunken parties...'"

**X.27.38**:
> "Late have I loved you, beauty so old and so new; late have I loved you."

#### Apologetic

| Work | Date | Content |
|------|------|---------|
| **City of God** (*De Civitate Dei*) | 413-426 | Christian philosophy of history (22 books) |

**City of God** structure:
- Books 1-10: Refute pagan claims that Christianity caused Rome's fall
- Books 11-22: Two cities (City of God vs. City of Man) from creation to eschaton

**Key themes**: Providence, history's meaning, justice, eternal destiny.

#### Anti-Pelagian

| Work | Target | Content |
|------|--------|---------|
| **On Nature and Grace** | Pelagius | Necessity of grace |
| **On the Spirit and the Letter** | Pelagianism | Grace enables obedience |
| **On Grace and Free Will** | Semi-Pelagianism | Both necessary |
| **On the Predestination of the Saints** | Semi-Pelagianism | Election |
| **On the Gift of Perseverance** | Semi-Pelagianism | Grace to persevere |

**Key Augustinian principles**:
- Human will wounded by Fall (not destroyed)
- Prevenient grace precedes any good work
- Faith itself is gift of grace
- Predestination to salvation

#### Anti-Donatist

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| **On Baptism** | Sacraments valid regardless of minister |
| Various letters and treatises | Church unity; sacramental validity |

**Key principle**: Sacraments work *ex opere operato*—by the work performed, not minister's holiness.

#### Anti-Manichean

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Against Faustus** | Detailed refutation |
| **On Free Will** | Evil as privation, not substance |
| **Confessions** (partly) | His Manichean past |

#### Exegetical

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| **On Genesis Literally Interpreted** | Creation; days as "ages"? |
| **Homilies on John** (124) | Extended commentary/sermons |
| **Homilies on 1 John** (10) | Love; theology of charity |
| **Expositions on the Psalms** | Complete psalter commentary |

#### Doctrinal

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| **On the Trinity** (*De Trinitate*) | 15 books; psychological analogies |
| **Enchiridion** | Faith, hope, love handbook |
| **On Christian Doctrine** | Biblical interpretation method |

**On the Trinity** key contributions:
- Psychological analogies: memory, understanding, will
- *Filioque* foundation: Spirit proceeds from Father *and Son*

**Enchiridion** (Handbook on Faith, Hope, and Love):
Concise summary of Christian doctrine organized around Creed, Lord's Prayer, love.

#### Other Important Works

| Work | Content |
|------|---------|
| **Letters** | 270+ surviving |
| **Sermons** | 400+ surviving |
| **Retractions** | Review of his own works |

### Key Theological Themes

| Theme | Position |
|-------|----------|
| **Grace** | Absolutely necessary; precedes faith; irresistible (for elect) |
| **Original sin** | Inherited guilt and concupiscence from Adam |
| **Predestination** | God's sovereign election; not based on foreseen merit |
| **Free will** | Real but wounded; needs grace to do good |
| **Church** | Visible society; mixed wheat and tares |
| **Sacraments** | Objective efficacy; minister's state irrelevant |
| **Trinity** | Psychological model; *Filioque* |
| **Scripture** | Inerrant; multiple senses; read with Church |

### Marian Content

Augustine on Mary:

**On Nature and Grace 36.42** (Sinlessness):
> "We must except the holy Virgin Mary, concerning whom I wish to raise no question when it touches the subject of sins, out of honor to the Lord."

**Sermon 186.1** (Perpetual virginity):
> "A virgin conceiving, a virgin bearing, a virgin pregnant, a virgin bringing forth, a virgin perpetual."

### Where to Find Full Text

- **New Advent**: newadvent.org/fathers (extensive Augustine collection)
- **CCEL**: NPNF1 Volumes 1-8 (devoted to Augustine)
- **Augustine's works**: augustinus.it (Latin and translations)
