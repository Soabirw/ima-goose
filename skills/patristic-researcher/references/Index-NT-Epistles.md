# New Testament Epistles Index

## Purpose

The NT Epistles are the **earliest Christian writings** we possess—some predating the Gospels in their final form. They represent the Apostolic teaching in its purest, most direct expression. This index catalogs these letters by author, with key themes and theological topics for guided research.

## Pauline Epistles

### Romans

**Date**: ~55-57 AD  
**Audience**: Church at Rome (mixed Jewish-Gentile)  
**Occasion**: Preparing for visit, establishing theological foundation

**Major Themes & Key Passages**:

| Theme | Key Passages | Notes |
|-------|--------------|-------|
| Justification by Faith | 3:21-31; 4:1-25; 5:1-2 | Central Pauline doctrine |
| Adam-Christ Typology | 5:12-21 | Foundational for soteriology |
| Baptismal Union with Christ | 6:1-14 | Dying and rising with Christ |
| Law and Grace | 7:1-25 | The struggle with sin |
| Life in the Spirit | 8:1-39 | No condemnation; Spirit's work |
| Election of Israel | 9:1-11:36 | Predestination, remnant, grafting |
| Transformed Living | 12:1-2 | Renewal of mind |
| Church as Body | 12:3-8 | Gifts and unity |
| Governing Authorities | 13:1-7 | Submission to rulers |
| Love Fulfills Law | 13:8-10 | Summary of ethics |
| Weak and Strong | 14:1-15:13 | Conscience and liberty |

**Patristic Usage**: Most commented NT book. Augustine's conversion (Rom 13:13-14). Foundational for Western soteriology.

```
"Catena Aurea" [passage]

# Ancient Christian Commentary on Scripture series
"Ancient Christian Commentary" [book] [topic]
```

### Connecting NT to Patristic Era

The NT letters were frequently quoted, commented upon, and used liturgically by the Fathers. Key connections:

| NT Theme | Patristic Development |
|----------|----------------------|
| Romans justification | Augustine vs. Pelagius |
| Philippians 2 kenosis | Christological councils |
| Ephesians 5:32 marriage | Sacramental theology |
| 1 Peter 3:21 baptism | Baptismal regeneration |
| James 5:14 anointing | Unction of the sick |
| 1 Tim 3:15 Church as pillar | Authority debates |
| 2 Peter 1:4 divine nature | Eastern theosis |
| Hebrews priesthood | Eucharistic sacrifice |
