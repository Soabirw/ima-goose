## Key Passages Every Researcher Should Know

### On the Eucharist

- **Didache 9-10**: Earliest Eucharistic prayers
- **Ignatius, Smyrnaeans 7**: "Flesh of our Savior Jesus Christ"
- **Justin, 1 Apology 65-67**: Sunday worship, bread and wine become flesh
- **Ambrose, De Sacramentis IV.14**: Words of Christ effect the change

### On the Church

- **1 Clement 42-44**: Apostolic succession
- **Ignatius, Smyrnaeans 8**: "Where the bishop is, there is the Church"
- **Cyprian, De Unitate 6**: "Cannot have God as Father without Church as Mother"
- **Augustine, City of God**: Two cities paradigm

### On Mary

- **Justin, Dialogue 100**: Eve-Mary parallel
- **Irenaeus, Against Heresies III.22.4**: "Knot of Eve's disobedience loosed"
- **Jerome, Against Helvidius**: Perpetual virginity defended
- **Augustine, On Nature and Grace 36.42**: Mary excepted from sin discussion

### On Christ

- **Ignatius, Ephesians 7.2**: "One Physician... God existing in flesh"
- **Irenaeus, Against Heresies V.1.1**: Recapitulation
- **Athanasius, On the Incarnation 54**: "He became man that we might become god"
- **Philippians 2:5-11**: Christ Hymn (NT foundation)

### On the Trinity

- **Tertullian, Against Praxeas**: "Three persons, one substance"
- **Gregory Naz., Oration 31**: Holy Spirit's divinity
- **Augustine, De Trinitate**: Psychological analogies

### On Grace

- **Romans 3-5**: Justification by faith
- **Augustine, anti-Pelagian works**: Grace precedes merit

## Red Flags in Research

### Watch For

| Issue | Example | Response |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Anachronism** | Using "transubstantiation" for Ignatius | Use period-appropriate language |
| **Disputed authorship** | Pseudo-Ignatius, Pseudo-Dionysius | Note uncertainty |
| **Montanist Tertullian** | Later rigorist works | Note period of writing |
| **Condemned Origen** | Some speculations condemned 553 | Distinguish opinions from reception |
| **Later interpolations** | Long recension of Ignatius | Use critical editions |

### Quality Control

- ✓ Cite specific sections, not just authors
- ✓ Note historical context
- ✓ Distinguish consensus from individual opinion
- ✓ Acknowledge development
- ✓ Verify with primary text when possible

## The Cloud of Witnesses

*"Therefore, since we are surrounded by so great a cloud of witnesses..."* (Hebrews 12:1)

From the Apostles through Augustine, these witnesses testify to the faith once delivered to the saints. Honor them by:

- **Reading them charitably** (they meant well)
- **Contextualizing them historically** (they faced real challenges)
- **Letting them speak** (quote them, don't just summarize)
- **Following their example** (scholarship in service of love)

*"The glory of God is a living man, and the life of man consists in beholding God."*
— Irenaeus, Against Heresies IV.20.7
